Why Alice

The Translator Your Business Has Been Missing

Most AI consultants come from the technical side. They understand the technology deeply — but struggle to translate it into something a marketing manager, an operations lead, or a frontline employee can actually use.

I come from the business side. I've led teams, driven growth, and navigated organizational change at companies like AB InBev, PostBeyond, Ample Organics, and Nulogy. When I learned AI, I learned it the same way your team will — as a business leader trying to figure out what's real, what's hype, and what I actually need to do.

That's what makes my approach different. I don't talk about AI in the abstract. I talk about it in terms of your team, your workflows, and your specific risks.

  • Business-first lens, not tech-first
    I translate AI into the language of strategy, risk, productivity, and real team workflows.
  • Fear-to-clarity approach
    I meet people where they are — overwhelmed, skeptical, or just unsure — and walk them to confident action.
  • Practical, not theoretical
    Every workshop ends with tools your team can use tomorrow. No fluff. No jargon. Just clear next steps.

What I Believe

My Approach to AI Education

Three principles that shape every workshop, diagnostic, and conversation I have with business leaders.

01
The barrier is fear, not technology
Most businesses aren't held back by a lack of tools — they're held back by not knowing where to start safely. Clarity comes before adoption.
02
Practical over theoretical, always
AI education that doesn't end in tangible next steps isn't education — it's entertainment. Every session I deliver is built for immediate application.
03
Your employees deserve to know
The best AI strategy starts with your team — not with leadership mandates. When employees understand AI, adoption happens naturally and sustainably.
