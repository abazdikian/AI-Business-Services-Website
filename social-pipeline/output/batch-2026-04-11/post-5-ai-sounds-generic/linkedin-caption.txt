Most people's AI sounds exactly like everyone else's.

That's not an accident.

Out of the box, ChatGPT and Claude are trained to be pleasant, neutral, and slightly eager to please. Fine for a quick search. Not fine when you're trying to get real work done — in your voice, with your standards, on your material.

Here's what I teach my clients in the first 30 minutes:

1. Teach AI what "good" looks like for YOU
Your tone. Your preferences. The way you phrase things. The standards you hold yourself to. Once Claude knows your defaults, it stops sounding like a stranger wrote it.

2. Kill the copy-paste bottleneck
Most entrepreneurs' AI workflow has a leak: copy from one tool → paste into AI → copy the answer → paste it somewhere else. You are the bottleneck. When you connect Claude directly to your tools, the time savings actually stick.

This is the difference between "I use AI sometimes" and "AI fits how I work."

It's not technical. It's personal.

And it takes 30 minutes to set up.
