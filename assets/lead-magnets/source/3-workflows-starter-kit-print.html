Workflow One

The Inbox Triage Workflow.

Bonus: Set up an Automated Daily Inbox Briefing with Claude Cowork

Turn 90 minutes a day on email into 15. One prompt classifies, drafts replies, and flags what's actually urgent.

Saves ≈ 5 hrs/week Setup: 15 min Tool: Claude.ai (Chat) · Pro $20/mo

You'll recognize this if…

  • You spend 60–120 minutes a day reading email and answering the same kinds of questions over and over.
  • You've tried "inbox zero" and it lasted 4 days.
  • You miss replies you meant to send because the email scrolled out of sight.
  • You write the same "thanks, I'll take a look" reply 8 times a day.

The exact prompt (copy-paste).

▶ Paste into Claude · Then paste 5–10 emails below it 
You are my email triage assistant. I'm pasting in 5–10 emails I haven't replied to.

For each email, do this in order:

1. CLASSIFY into one of: REPLY-NEEDED · FYI · ARCHIVE · DELEGATE
2. RANK urgency: HIGH (today), MEDIUM (this week), LOW (whenever)
3. If REPLY-NEEDED: draft a 2–3 sentence reply in my voice (warm, direct, no "I hope this finds you well")
4. If anything is time-sensitive (deadline, meeting, payment), flag it at the top with ⚠

Output as a clean table I can scan in 30 seconds:

| # | From | Subject | Class | Urgency | Suggested reply (if any) |

End with a one-line summary: "X to reply to today, Y to skim, Z to archive."

EMAILS:
[paste below]

What it looks like running.

Step 1. Paste the prompt into a fresh Claude chat. Claude reads the rules and waits for your inputs.

Step 2. Claude triages your real emails into a clean table with class, urgency, and suggested replies. ≈ 60–90 seconds for 8 emails.

How to know it's working.

  • Day 1: 8 emails triaged in under a minute. You only write 2 replies from scratch instead of 8.
  • Day 3: You check email twice a day instead of every 20 minutes. Your "to-reply" list lives in Claude, not your head.
  • Week 2: The "what was I supposed to follow up on?" anxiety stops. ≈ 5 hours back per week.
⚡ Make it stickier Save the prompt as a Claude Project (Pro). Now you can paste the emails straight in without re-pasting the prompt. Add your business voice as a memory ("I run a 6-person dental practice; reply with that context") and Claude tunes drafts to your tone forever.
⚠ The honest caveat Don't auto-send drafts. Read each one before clicking send. AI is good at ≈ 80% of replies; the last 20% is still you reading the tone of the relationship. Five seconds per draft beats five minutes typing it from scratch.

Workflow Two

The SOP-to-AI-Trainer Workflow.

Stop re-explaining your business every time you talk to AI. Train it once, with a doc you already have. Reuse it forever.

Saves ≈ 3 hrs/week Setup: 30 min, once Tool: Claude.ai (Projects) · Pro $20/mo

You'll recognize this if…

  • Every time you ask AI for help, you re-paste the same context (your business, your customers, your offers, your voice).
  • You've asked AI for "an email like the one I sent last week" and it had no idea what you meant.
  • You have an SOP doc, an onboarding guide, or a brand voice doc sitting in Google Drive you haven't opened in 4 months.
  • You think "if I could just clone my business knowledge into AI once", that's exactly what this does.

The exact setup (30 minutes, once).

  1. Open Claude.ai. Click the "Projects" sidebar (Pro feature, $20/mo).
  2. Create a project called "[Your Biz] Knowledge Base."
  3. Upload your existing docs: SOP / onboarding guide / brand voice / pricing / FAQ. PDFs, Google Docs (export as PDF), or .txt all work.
  4. Paste this system prompt in the project's "Custom instructions" box (see prompt below).
  5. Start a new chat in that project. From now on, every message uses your docs as context automatically.
smallbusinessaicoach.com Workflow 02 · SOP-to-AI-Trainer · Page 05

The system prompt.

▶ Paste into the project's "Custom instructions" 
You are my AI assistant for [BUSINESS NAME].
The attached docs describe how my business actually runs:
who my customers are, what I sell, my brand voice, my pricing,
my SOPs, and what I will NEVER do.

Rules for every reply:

1. Use the docs as the source of truth. If something contradicts
   the docs, the docs win.
2. Match my brand voice from the writing samples. Specifically:
   [warm, direct, plain-language; no jargon; no "in today's
   fast-paced world"; no "leverage"; no "synergy"].
3. If I ask for something the docs don't cover, say so before
   guessing.
4. When I ask for a draft (email, post, proposal), output it
   ready to send/publish, not a 500-word explanation of how to
   write it.
5. If the request involves money, contracts, or legal, flag it
   and tell me to verify before sending.

What it looks like running.

Step 1. Paste the system prompt into the Project's Custom Instructions. Claude now answers every chat in this project with your docs as the source of truth.

Step 2. Claude locks in your rules and asks what you want to work on. From now on, every reply uses your business as context — no re-pasting.

  • Week 1: Replies sound like YOU wrote them. You stop deleting AI's "I hope this finds you well" preambles.
  • Week 2: You ask "draft a follow-up to the proposal I sent the Tuesday client" and Claude knows what you mean.

Workflow Three

The Weekly Marketing Loop.

5 reel scripts and a newsletter outline from one prompt. Built around your brand voice. Repeats every week with a single click.

Saves ≈ 4 hrs/week Setup: 15 min Tool: Claude.ai (Chat) · Pro $20/mo

You'll recognize this if…

  • You know you "should" be posting more, but content is the bottleneck.
  • You've spent a Sunday afternoon staring at a blank doc trying to think of 5 reel ideas.
  • Your last newsletter went out 6 weeks ago. You meant to write one. It just didn't happen.
  • You'd rather record reels than write the scripts.

The exact prompt.

▶ Paste into Claude (or your SOP-trained project from Workflow 02) 
You are my content strategist. Generate this week's content
in one pass.

CONTEXT (the only thing I need to give you each week):
- This week's main topic: [TOPIC]
- The specific pain it solves for my audience: [PAIN]
- The product/offer I'm warming people up to: [OFFER]

Output, in order:

1. FIVE short-form video scripts (15–30 sec each).
   Each one: a single hook line, a single beat, a single CTA.
   Different angles: a confessional, a contrarian, a checklist,
   a story, a stat-based one.
   Format each as: HOOK / SCRIPT / CTA.

2. ONE newsletter outline:
   - Subject line (under 50 chars, no clickbait, no emojis)
   - Opening line (1 sentence, hook)
   - Body (3 short paragraphs, plain language)
   - One link (to the OFFER) in the natural closing.

Write everything in MY voice (from the project docs). Plain
language. No "in today's fast-paced world." No emojis in body
copy.
smallbusinessaicoach.com Workflow 03 · Weekly Marketing Loop · Page 08

What it looks like running.

Step 1. Paste the prompt with this week's TOPIC, PAIN, and OFFER filled in. The only thing that changes week to week is those 3 lines.

Step 2. Claude returns 5 reel scripts (Confessional, Contrarian, Checklist, Story, Stat) plus a newsletter outline, all in your voice. ≈ 2–3 minutes the first run.

How to know it's working.

  • Week 1: You record 5 reels in one sitting using the scripts. You ship the newsletter on time.
  • Week 3: You stop "not knowing what to post." The bottleneck moves from ideation to recording.
  • Month 2: Your audience starts replying "your content has been on fire lately." It has. You finally have a rhythm.
⚡ The leverage move After every weekly run, paste your top-performing post BACK into Claude with the note "Why did this one outperform?" Over 4–6 weeks, your prompt + your project docs become a self-tuning content engine. This is also exactly what we build live in the workshop.

Bonus Workflow · Surprise

The Invoice Killer.

For the small business owner who hand-types the same client invoice every month. Set it up once. Claude runs it on the 1st, every month, on its own.

Saves 2–4 hrs/month Setup: 15 min, once Tool: Claude Cowork (Pro feature · in desktop app)

This one's different. The first three workflows you trigger by hand. This one you set up once, and then Claude Cowork runs it on a schedule, on its own, while you sleep. Cowork is a Pro feature inside the Claude desktop app (not the web Chat) that lets Claude run scheduled tasks for you.

You'll recognize this if…

  • You invoice the same client(s) for roughly the same services every month.
  • The 1st of the month rolls around and you're cross-referencing last month's hours, services, and totals from memory or scattered notes.
  • You've forgotten to send an invoice on time, more than once.
  • Tax math, line items, totals, and a polished PDF, every single month, is "just admin" eating 2–4 hours.

The 15-minute setup.

  1. Open the Claude desktop app and click the Cowork tab at the top (next to Chat and Code). If you don't see Cowork, update the desktop app, it ships with Pro.
  2. Create a new Project called "Invoicing." Drop in last month's invoice PDFs and any service rate-sheet you use, so Claude has your real numbers.
  3. Paste the instructions below into the project's Instructions panel. Edit the bracketed bits ([client name], [services], [your tax rate]) for your business.
  4. Schedule it. In the Scheduled section, set it to run at 9am on day 1 of every month. Cowork will ask you the questions, you approve, it generates the PDF.
smallbusinessaicoach.com Bonus Workflow 04 · The Invoice Killer · Page 11

The exact instructions (paste into your Cowork project).

▶ Paste into the Instructions panel of your "Invoicing" Cowork project 
Run the monthly invoicing routine for [CLIENT NAME].
Follow these steps every time:

1. Determine the invoicing month: it's the month that just
   ended (the previous calendar month). For example, if today
   is June 1, invoice for May.

2. Ask me how many hours of each service I delivered last
   month. List the services I offer:
   - [Service A] @ [rate]/hr
   - [Service B] @ [rate]/hr
   - [Service C] @ [rate]/hr

3. Calculate subtotal, tax at [your tax rate]%, and total.
   Show me the math. Wait for my approval before proceeding.

4. Once I approve, generate a clean PDF invoice with:
   - My business name, address, contact (use the rate-sheet
     in this project)
   - Invoice number = [PREFIX]-YYYY-MM (e.g. INV-2026-05)
   - Invoice date = today
   - Due date = today + 30 days
   - Line items, subtotal, tax, total
   - Bank/payment details from the rate-sheet
   - Polite "thanks for your business" footer

5. Save the PDF to this project as
   "[CLIENT]-invoice-YYYY-MM.pdf" and notify me it's ready.

Always do this on the 1st of every month. If I don't respond
within 24 hours, send me a reminder.

What it looks like running, inside Claude Cowork.

Step 1 · The Cowork tab in the Claude desktop app. Create an "Invoicing" project here, separate from regular Chat. This is where the monthly run lives.

Step 2 · Inside the project: instructions on the right, scheduled task ("AI pro monthly invoice · day 1 of the month") wired up. Cowork runs this on its own.

Step 3 · The scheduled task itself, "Active · Next run: Jun 1 at 9:02 AM." That's the whole product. You set it up once. Cowork runs it forever.

⚠ The honest caveat Cowork still asks before sending. It doesn't email your client without your approval, and it shouldn't. Use it as a "10-minute monthly check-in" with Claude instead of a 2-hour invoicing chore. You still hit Send on the invoice.
⚡ Stack it with Workflow 02 Drop your service rate-sheet, payment terms, and "how I write to clients" voice samples into the same Cowork project. Now the monthly invoice email comes out in your tone, with your rates, with your bank details, every single month. No edits needed.
